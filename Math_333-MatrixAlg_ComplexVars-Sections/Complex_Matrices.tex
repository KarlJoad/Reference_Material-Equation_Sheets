\section{Complex Matrices}\label{sec:Complex_Matrices}
Very simply a complex matrix is a \nameref{def:Matrix} that has complex-valued entries, $a_{i, j} \in \ComplexNumbers$.

\begin{definition}[Complex Matrix]\label{def:Complex_Matrix}
  A \emph{complex matrix} is a \nameref{def:Matrix} where one more more of its entries are complex valued.
  This means that in $A_{n \by m} . \exists a_{i, j} \in \ComplexNumbers$.
\end{definition}
Complex matrices retain the same syntax for definition.
Throughout this section, we will be referring to the generic comples matrix $V_{1 \by 2}$.
